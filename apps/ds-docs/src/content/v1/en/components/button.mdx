## Overview

Buttons are interactive elements that trigger actions when clicked or tapped. They communicate actions that users can take and are typically placed throughout your UI, from forms to dialogs to navigation.

The Button component provides a consistent, accessible way to trigger actions throughout your application.

## When to Use

Use buttons to:

- Trigger primary actions (Submit, Save, Continue)
- Navigate to different views or pages
- Toggle states or settings
- Trigger secondary actions (Cancel, Delete)
- Submit forms

<Callout type="tip" title="Choosing the right variant">
  Use **primary** for the main action, **secondary** for alternative actions, **destructive** for dangerous actions, and **ghost** for less prominent actions.
</Callout>

## Do's and Don'ts

<DoDont title="Usage guidelines">
  <template v-slot:do>
    <p class="text-sm mb-2">Use buttons for actionable items:</p>
    <button class="px-4 py-2 bg-primary-500 text-white rounded-md text-sm">Submit Form</button>
  </template>
  <template v-slot:dont>
    <p class="text-sm mb-2">Don't use buttons for navigation:</p>
    <button class="px-4 py-2 bg-primary-500 text-white rounded-md text-sm">Go to Dashboard</button>
    <p class="text-xs text-red-600 mt-1">Use a link instead</p>
  </template>
</DoDont>

<DoDont>
  <template v-slot:do>
    <p class="text-sm mb-2">Use clear, action-oriented labels:</p>

  </template>

  </template>
</DoDont>
